Overview

Federal law defines child pornography as any visual depiction of sexually explicit conduct involving a minor, and the United STates Department of Justice may prosecute offenses occurring across state of international borders and almost any offense involving the Internet.
Though their laws were created to protect minors from explotation, states are prosecuting minors under child pornography statutes for sending nude or other lurid self-portraits, even when the minors sends the photos without coercion. It is important to distinguish child pornography from the conventional understanding of the term pornography. It is a form of child sexual exploitation, and each image graphically sexually memorializes the sexaul abuse of that child.
