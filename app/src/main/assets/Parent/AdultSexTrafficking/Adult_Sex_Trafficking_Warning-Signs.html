Indicators of Risk

At the start of the grooming process, young people often exhibit behavior that can alert family, friends, and professionals that they may be at risk of being sexually exploited. As with the case in children and adolescents, some warning signs of abuse are:

  • A person suddenly has too much money, and no way to explain it. Perhaps they have new clothes, nails, hairstyles, etc.
  • New tattoos, especially of a person's name or a gang brand.
  • Bruises, scratches, and cuts
  • Signs they may be cutting themselves
  • Trouble sleeping
  • Goes to school, but does not stay
  • Attempts to run away from home or sometimes runs for a few days and returns
  • Having an older boyfriend or girlfriend. Protects boyfriend/girlfriend even though abused
  • Lying about their age or having a false ID. May have prepaid, burner or generic phones
  • Sexually explicit profiles on social networking websites
  • Texts using the words "Daddy" or "Wifey". Keep the phone. Do not erase, and take to the police.
  • Changes in Appetite
  • Overly mature behavior
  • Involvement in drug/alcohol use
  • A sudden deterioration of performance in school or dropping out
