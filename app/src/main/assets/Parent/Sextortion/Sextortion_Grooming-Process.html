How Predator's "Groom"

Predators target vulnerable children and execute a physiological grooming process aimed to make their targets dependent upon them. Predators are skilled at physical and psychological manipulation in order to gain their victim’s trust.

1. Targeting a Victim❯
Social media is a growing emotional outlet that minors use to express themselves to their peers. Predators understand this and target these vulnerable children and high school-aged kids who may be susceptible to their process.
2. Gaining Their Trust❯
Online predators will attempt to gain their victim’s trust through casual conversations. Often times, they will pose as other children their age to gather information. Over time, the victim will believe they have made a trustworthy online “friend” - without even meeting them in person.
3. Filling a need❯
The trafficker now has sensitive information about the victim’s life. They will attempt to make their victim’s fall dependent upon them by giving them gifts, being a friend, or being in a love relationship.
The First Step❯
Once an online predator has gained their victim’s trust and dependency, they begin to isolate their victim from their friends and family.
5. Abuse Begins❯
The predator now will express that their services of charming love and friendship need to be repaid. This is typically in the form of sexual services.
