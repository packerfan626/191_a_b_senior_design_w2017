Preventative Measures

There are some measures one can take in order to prevent against becoming a victim of human trafficking. One of them is to be aware of how traffickers recruit people. Traffickers make false promises of a better life. They paint unrealistic pictures of what life could be like with lots of money. In the case of labor trafficking, if the employer makes the job sound very appealing and makes promises that are uncommon or unusual for the position you are applying for, ask for another person’s opinion before signing any contracts. In the case of sex trafficking, they quickly befriend a person showering them with gifts and displays of affection, particularly recruiters who will later force a girl into prostitution.

If someone, whether stranger or acquaintance, promises something that seems too good in return for sex or free work, wait. Listen to the intuitive voice inside your head, check with family and friends for advice. Do Internet searches or background checks on the person wanting you to go with them. Say no and see how they react. Look for signs of abusive or possessive behaviors. Is the person trying to isolate or turn you against family and friends? If so, avoid that person.
